# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Students and faculty at UNSA (Universidad Nacional de San Agustín). They access the system during election periods to cast their votes. Sessions are short, focused, and task-driven — the user wants to authenticate, vote, and see results in under a minute. They access from laptops and phones, often in between classes or from faculty offices.

## Product Purpose

A distributed electronic voting system that lets UNSA voters cast ballots securely in university elections (rector, council, dean). The system ensures vote immutability, auditability, and high availability through a microservices architecture. Success means a voter can complete the full flow — login, select an election, choose a candidate, confirm, and view results — without confusion or delay.

## Brand Personality

Modern, Transparent, Accessible. The interface communicates institutional trustworthiness without being cold or bureaucratic. It feels open, civic, and approachable — like a well-designed government service, not a corporate dashboard.

## Anti-references

- **Corporate dark themes.** No dark navbars, no black backgrounds, no SaaS-dashboard aesthetic. This is a civic tool, not an enterprise admin panel.
- **Gamified or playful UI.** Voting is a serious act — no confetti, no badges, no celebratory animations.
- **Over-designed complexity.** No decorative flourishes, gradient text, glass morphism, or side-stripe borders. Every element earns its place by helping the voter move forward.

## Design Principles

1. **Frictionless voting.** The primary flow (login → vote → confirm → results) must be obvious and take no more than 4 taps/clicks. Every extra step loses voters.
2. **Institutional clarity, not corporate polish.** Clean enough to inspire trust, warm enough to feel like a university service, never slick enough to feel like a vendor product.
3. **Spanish-first.** All UI text, labels, and errors must be in Spanish. The interface is designed for the language it speaks, not translated after the fact.
4. **Mobile-ready by default.** Many students vote from phones. Every screen works on a 375px viewport without horizontal scroll or tiny tap targets.
5. **Feedback on every action.** Every click produces immediate, visible feedback — loading states, confirmations, error messages. The voter never wonders "did it work?"

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA compliance. Sufficient color contrast on all text. Form labels and error messages are clear. The flow works without relying on color alone (status indicators use text + icon + color).
